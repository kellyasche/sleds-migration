This dataset provides each CTE course taken by a student. This means that there were 226,487 instances of a student taking a CTE course. There are 18 columns providing the PersonID taking the course, the fiscal year, the K12 organization, along with details about the actual course itself.

There are two goals with this dataset.

  1. Format it so each observation represents a single PersonID along with the total number of CTE courses they took.
  2. Format it so each observation represents a single PersonID along with the number of CTE courses taken in each career field.

I could use career cluster but there are 20 different career clusters. The career fields are more broad and there are only 6 categories.

There seems to be a minor coding error in the careerfield column. For one particular class, it was consistently labeled “Information Technology” even though the field requires a number value. In addition, “information Technology” doesn’t seem to match the course due to the following information;

Program Course Name: Family and Consumer Science Course title: Apparrel CareerCluster: Design CareerFieldName: 6 (which should be a letter value, not a code).

Due to this information, I recoded anything that was “Information Technology” for that particular course as a “6”.

After doing this, the dataset has all individuals/graduates with a column for each CTE course careerfield containing the number of courses they took within that CareerField. I will summarise below to see how many students took courses within each CareerField.

Summary - Number of CTE courses taken by a student


We’ll start with summarising the number of cte courses the observations have taken.

The tables below show that the average number of CTE courses taken by all PersonID in the master dataset is 5.69 with a standard deviation of +/-4.95. This is a high standard deviation meaning there are values quite far from the mean.

The second table below shows the average number of CTE courses taken by all PersonID that have taken at least one CTE course was 6.29 with a standard deviation of +/-4.82. So the standard deviation decreases a bit with this population likely because we got rid of all the “0” values that are in the full dataset. But not significant.



Below is a chart showing the distribution of CTE courses taken by individuals. This shows that a a large majority of students take 10 courses or less.



Up next is to see if the percentage of students taking a CTE course varies significantly by RUCA group.

There is a significant difference in whether a student has taken at least one CTE course and their high school county RUCA category. significant variation by RUCA group. Although 90.3% of all graduates took at least one CTE course, that percentage is highest in entirely rural counties and decreases as counties become more urban.

Of those students that took at least 1 CTE course, the average number of CTE courses taken ranged from 5.4 in urban/town/rural mix counties to 6.5 in town/rural mix county group.

Summary - Career field of CTE courses taken

Let’s dig into the breadth of CTE course concentrations a graduate would take by looking at the proportion of graduates that took at least one course within a concentration.

The table below shows that over half of the graduates took at least one course in business, management, and administration; ag, food and natural resources; and human services.

From there, the percentage drops off quite a bit with engineering, manufacturing, and technology next with 19% and arts, communications and information systems with 7%. Health Science Technology is 1%.



Now we will take a look at the breakdown of the number of times a CTE courses has been taken within a career field for each PersonID. I expect there to be a lot of variation in this data since different schools might focus on different types of CTE offerings depending on the local employment makeup.

First, lets look at the concentrations of courses taken across career fields.

Not surprisingly, Agriculture, Food & Natural Resources was the careerfield with the highest percentage of courses taken with 34.9%. This was followed by business, management & administration with 24.9% and human services with 20.6% of CTE courses taken in that career field.



Next we will see if there’s any significant differences by region.

There is one significant difference when looking at the breakdown of CTE courses taken within careerfield by EDR. EDR 8 - Southwest had a significantly higher percentage of courses taken within the Agriculture, Food & Natural Resources career field with 44.1% compared to 28.7% and 26.4% in EDR 6E and 6W respectively.

There was also a significantly lower percentage of courses taken in Engineering, Manufacturing and Technology with 4.0% of courses taken compared to 13.7% in both EDR 6E and 6W.


Lets see how this looks broken down by county.

The county maps do show some differences in percentages. A significantly higher percentage of CTE courses taken were in Jackson (94.6%), Pipestone (65.9%) Cottonwood (56.5%), and Lincoln (53.8%) counties.

There were also higher percentages of courses taken in business, management and administration in Big Stone (54.4%), Murray (43.4%), and Renville (40.4%) counties.



The next big question is whether these percentages vary by year. Are there any trends?

This is honestly a bit surprising. The chart raises a couple of questions.

There was a significant decrease in the NA categorized courses taken. With this was a large increase in the percentage of students taking Ag, food and natural resouces courses. The remaining courses pretty much remained the same.
