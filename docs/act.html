There are 33,047 observations in the dataset. Each observation is an ACT test taken by a student in Southwest Minnesota. The unique feature here is the time it was taken. This means that a student could have taken the ACT twice and their PersonID would show up as two observations.

Out of the scores provided, I’m just going to use the composite score. Since an individual may have taken the test multiple times I will only use the highest composite score for each individual. This is how colleges do it as well.

Summary of ACT data

First, lets see what percentage of observations took the ACT and what the average composite score was.

The table below shows that nearly two-thirds of graduates from Southwest Minnesota have taken the ACT at some point in the dataset.



Next lets see if the ACT taken rate differs across RUCA, regions and counties.

The cross-tabs for the percentage of students who have taken the ACT by RUCA category has a p-value of less than .01, indicating that RUCA category is related. However, the percentage of students graduating from high schools in counties within these RUCA groups are very close. Nearly two-thirds of students across all RUCA groups take the ACT exam. The significant difference seems to be in the higher percentages in the entirely rural and urban/town/rural mix county group, which are closer to 70% of all students.
